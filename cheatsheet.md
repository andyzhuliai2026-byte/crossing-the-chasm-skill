# Cheatsheet — Crossing the Chasm

## The Technology Adoption Life Cycle, at a glance

| Segment | Label | % | Buys for | Pricing | Channel | References |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Innovators | Tech enthusiasts | 2.5% | Technology itself | N/A | Direct / self-service | None — they validate to others |
| Early adopters | Visionaries | 13.5% | Order-of-magnitude strategic leap | **Value-based** (will pay UP) | Direct sales | Each other |
| Early majority | **Pragmatists** | 34% | Productivity improvement | **Competition-based** (~30% market-leader premium) | Direct / Sales 2.0 / VAR | Other pragmatists, vertically |
| Late majority | Conservatives | 34% | Established standard, low risk | **Cost-based** (commodity) | VAR / self-service / retail | Mainstream consensus |
| Laggards | Skeptics | 16% | Don't buy unless embedded | N/A | N/A | N/A |

**The Chasm = gap between Visionaries and Pragmatists.**

## What changes when you cross the chasm

| Dimension | Pre-chasm (visionary) | Post-chasm (pragmatist) |
|---|---|---|
| Buying motive | Change agent / 10× strategic leap | Productivity improvement |
| Risk tolerance | High (wants to be first) | Low (wants to be safe) |
| References | Doesn't need them | Demands them, vertically |
| Whole product | They'll build it themselves | **You** must assemble it |
| Pricing | Value-based, price-insensitive | Competition-based, leader premium |
| Sales motion | Project-based, custom | Standardized, repeatable |
| Positioning rhetoric | Product-centric | **Market-centric** |
| Competition | Status quo (no real competitors) | Visible competitive set required |
| Org persona | Pioneer | Settler |
| Revenue curve | Sharp spike | Plateau then staircase |

## Beachhead score sheet (Market Development Strategy Checklist)

Score each candidate 1–5 on:

**Showstoppers (low score kills the candidate)**
- [ ] Target Customer — single identifiable economic buyer, accessible, funded
- [ ] Compelling Reason to Buy — pain so severe they can't wait another year
- [ ] Whole Product — fieldable in 3 months, dominant in 12
- [ ] Competition — no one has crossed the chasm here ahead of you

**Nice-to-have (recoverable with time/money)**
- [ ] Partners and Allies
- [ ] Distribution
- [ ] Pricing
- [ ] Positioning
- [ ] Next Target Customer (bowling-alley potential)

Best total: 9 × 5 = 45. Showstopper subtotal floor: any "low" on a showstopper = disqualify.

## Two-sentence positioning template

> **For** [target customer — beachhead only] <br>
> **Who are dissatisfied with** [the current *market alternative*] <br>
> **Our product is a** [product category] <br>
> **That provides** [compelling reason to buy]. <br>
> **Unlike** [the *product alternative*], <br>
> **We have assembled** [key whole-product features for your specific application].

If you can't say it during one elevator ride, it's not a strategy yet.

## The Whole Product Model (four layers)

| Layer | Definition | Pre-chasm | Post-chasm |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generic | What's in the box | Hero | Necessary but not sufficient |
| Expected | Minimum complete configuration | Visionaries provide it | **You** provide it |
| Augmented | Maximum support for the buying objective | Optional | Required |
| Potential | Room for third-party extensions | N/A | Market-leader signal |

## The four gears (consumer-digital alternative — Appendix 2)

Order matters: **Engage first → then Acquire → then Enlist → Monetize last.**

| Gear | What it does | Wrong if invoked too early |
|---|---|---|
| Acquire | Onboard new users/content | Starves engagement; leaky bucket |
| Engage | Compelling repeatable experience | (Must be first) |
| Enlist | Convert hyper-fans to evangelists | Empty evangelism with no engaged base |
| Monetize | Extract revenue | Stalls the other three gears |

## Quick decision rules

- **"Should we target more than one niche?"** No.
- **"Should we trust the analyst's TAM number?"** No.
- **"Should we let the channel push back on price?"** Yes — they're usually right.
- **"Should we pay our evangelists?"** No — kills the dynamic.
- **"Should we monetize early in a consumer digital play?"** No — stalls engagement.
- **"Should we adopt profitability discipline early?"** Yes — preserves equity, forces focus.
- **"Should we plan revenue as a hockey stick?"** No — plan a staircase.
- **"Is our competitor smaller than us?"** Doesn't matter; what matters is *fortified hill* — never attack a defended beachhead.

## Common diagnostic phrases (what they actually mean)

| What you hear | What's really going on |
|---|---|
| "Great presentation!" (no PO) | No compelling reason to buy |
| "We'd need more references" | Visionary references don't transfer to pragmatist; in the chasm |
| "It's a commodity now" | UX of the product has been commoditized; market is in the second crack |
| "Our market is $10B by 2026" | House of cards; exit gracefully |
| "We have no competition" | Pragmatists hear: "this isn't a real market" |
| "We just need better marketing" | Often: wrong segment, wrong channel, or no whole product |
